NaF vs KF

Type: IONIC SOLID

Sodium fluoride and potassium fluoride are both ionic solids with an octahedral arrangement in their cubic body lattice structures

BONDING

Ionic solids are held together by strong ionic bonds involding the electrostatic forces of attraction between the oppositely charged ions. These ions are created as the metal loses electrons to become a cation and the non-metal will gain those electrons to become an anion

Bond Strength: FACTORS

  1. Ionic Charge

    Both compounds have the same ionic charge as they are both composed of one 1+ ion and one 1- ion.

  2. Interionic distance

    This is the primary differentiating factor between the bond strengths of KF and NaF. While both compounds have F- as the anion, K has 3 shells as an ion while Na as an ion only has 2 shells and hence is smaller, leading to a reduced distance between the ions and a higher charge density, resulting in NaF having stronger bonds.
