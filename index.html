NaF vs KF

Type: IONIC SOLID

Both sodium fluoride and potassium fluoride are both ionic solids with an octahedral arrangement in their lattice structures

BONDING

Ionic solids are held together by strong ionic bonds involding the electrostatic forces of attraction between the oppositely charged ions

Bond Strength: FACTORS

  1. Ionic Charge

    Both compounds have the same ionic charge as they are both composed of one 1+ ion and one 1- ion.

  2. Interionic distance

    This is the primary differentiating factor between the strengths of KF and NaF. While both compounds have F- as the anion, K has 3 shells as an ion (K+) while Na only has 2 shells (Na+) and hence is smaller, leading to a reduced distance between the ions, resulting in NaF having stronger bonds.
